## Appearances

- [[katherine_jarmul_privacy]] — Guest speaker; delivered comprehensive talk on privacy in AI covering definitions, guardrail taxonomies, red teaming, federated learning, and practical privacy engineering
- [[william_horton_maven_clinic]] — Referenced for privacy talk; her guardrail taxonomy maps to Maven's input guardrails

## Key Contributions

- Provided a three-layer guardrail taxonomy: external deterministic (software-based filters), external algorithmic (separate classifier models like LlamaGuard), and internal alignment training (RLHF, fine-tuning) -- the most structured guardrail breakdown in the course
- Established that system prompts should be treated as public because they can and will be exfiltrated, so never put anything in them you would not put on your website
- Framed privacy as a spectrum (not binary on/off), where any step toward more privacy for the same utility is valuable
- Advocated organizing red teaming as creative hack days involving the whole organization rather than relegating it to an overloaded security team
