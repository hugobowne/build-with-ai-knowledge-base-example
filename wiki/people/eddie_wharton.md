## Appearances

- [[next_level_evals_stella_eddie]] — Guest speaker; co-presented with [[stella_liu]] on causal inference in AI evaluation, LLM judge calibration, and practical eval methodology

## Key Contributions

- Introduced causal inference as the next frontier of AI evaluation, including calibrating cheap LLM judges to expensive human expert labels, quantifying uncertainty, and power analysis for label budgets
- Created CJ (Causal Judge), an open-source package for calibrating LLM judges to human experts (10K+ PyPI downloads)
- Framed both offline and online evaluation as fundamentally "policy evaluation" -- the same problem whether testing a drug in an RCT, a red vs. green button, or prompt A vs. prompt B
- Identified the biggest eval mistake as "standing up evals without actually looking at your data" -- hypothesizing what matters without examining real user interactions

## Notable Quotes

- "Standing up evals without actually looking at your data... hypothesizing what matters without actually looking at the interactions that users are having with your system." — [[next_level_evals_stella_eddie]]
- "The person who's best suited to thinking about evaluation... someone who has really good intuition for the product and has really strong empathy for the user." — [[next_level_evals_stella_eddie]]
