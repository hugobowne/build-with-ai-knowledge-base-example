## Appearances

- [[natalia_ines_guest_workshop]] — Guest speaker; delivered talk on annotation best practices, label scheme design, cognitive load reduction, and when to use LLMs versus classic NLP

## Key Contributions

- Presented the principle that factoring business logic from classification logic is the single most impactful annotation design decision, illustrated with crime report extraction and custody document anonymization case studies
- Demonstrated that making multiple single-label annotation passes is counterintuitively faster (10x in the S&P Global case) than one multi-label pass, due to cognitive load reduction
- Argued that LLMs should be used to build NLP systems (writing rules, generating training data) rather than being the production system, when speed, privacy, cost, or accuracy at scale matter
- Introduced structured RAG (information extraction into schemas first, then retrieval on structured data) as an alternative to naive vector database approaches
