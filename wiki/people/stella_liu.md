## Appearances

## Key Contributions

- Framed evals as both a compass for product development and a pass/fail gate for releases, adding the compliance dimension from regulated industries (education)
- Argued that hallucination exists on a spectrum: possibly desirable in writing tools ("I don't want the writing to be deterministic"), definitely bad in factual retrieval
- Established that if AI evals could be fully automated by AI, we would not have an evals problem in the first place -- human judgment remains essential
- Emphasized that product requirements documents are often missing because teams jump from prototyping to production without defining what the product is designed NOT to do
